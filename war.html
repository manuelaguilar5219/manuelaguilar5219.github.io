WAR

What does WAR mean?

Wins Above Replacement (WAR) is an attempt by the sabermetric baseball community to summarize a player's total contributions to their team in one statistic. It values everything a player does like Hitting, Base Running, and Defense. There are two main versions of WAR that most people use. The two versions are FWAR and BWAR. FWAR is calculated by Fangraphs and BWAR is calculated by Baseball reference. They are meant to be the same but both sites calculate them slightly different. Most people tend to use FWAR for Position Players and BWAR for Pitchers. A replacement level player(A baseball player who performs at a level that's expected from a player who's acquired at minimal cost to a team.) will have 0-1 WAR in a season, an Average starting caliber player will have 2-3 WAR in a season, an All Star level player will have 4-5 WAR in a season, and an MVP level player will have 6+ WAR in a season. Certain players will get a boost in WAR numbers due to the position that they play for example, Shortstops, Catchers, and Center Fielders get more WAR from defense because those are the most important positions on the field. Average defense at Shortstop is more valuable than average defense at Second Base so, that is why certain positions may earn more WAR even if their other stats may look similar. Players who play DH(Designated Hitter) get penalized in WAR due to the fact that they don't play defense on the field and only hit.

What is the difference between FWAR and BWAR?

The biggest difference between the two versions of WAR is how they calculate defensive value and pitching. For defense Fangraphs uses UZR(Ultimate Zone Rating) to calculate defense and Baseball Reference uses DRS(Defensive Runs Saved). Fangraphs values defense more than Baseball Reference. For pitchers Fangraphs calculates WAR by the pitchers FIP(Fielding Independent Pitching) and Baseball Reference uses RA9(Runs Allowed Per 9 Innings).
