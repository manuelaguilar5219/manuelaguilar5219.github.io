wRC+

What is wRC+?

Weighted Runs Created Plus (wRC+) is a stat that measures how a player's offensive production compares to the league average player. League average for position players is 100, and every point above 100 is a percentage point above league average. For example, a 125 wRC+ means a player is 25% better than the league average hitter. Similarly, every point below 100 is a percentage point below league average, so a 80 wRC+ means a player is 20% worse than the league average hitter. wRC+ also measures how a park effects a player's production for example, a player who plays in Colorado will have their wRC+ negatively impacted due to them playing in a hitter friendly park.

How is wRC+ calculated?

Calculating wRC+ can be very complicated so the best way to explain how this works is to walk through each of the steps. First we have wRAA/PA, which measures the number of runs above average a player contributes to his team at the plate per plate appearance. Another way to arrive at wRAA/PA is to simply take a player's wOBA minus the League wOBA and divide it by the wOBA Scale. Next we have league average runs per plate appearance, this is simply the MLB Runs divided by the total number of plate appearances across the game during that season. After that we have the park adjustment, here we are essentially calculating how many runs per plate appearance we should add or subtract from a player's total based on their home environment. To do so, we take MLB average R/PA and subtract out the MLB average R/PA times the park factor. To properly use the park factor, you should take the number listed on our park factor page and divide it by 100. So a 98 park factor should be used as 0.98 in this equation. After we add all of those numbers together, you divide them by the specific league wRC/PA.
