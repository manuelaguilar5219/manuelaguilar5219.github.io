wRC+

What does wRC+ mean?

Weighted Runs Created Plus (wRC+) is a stat that measures how a player's offensive production compares to the league average player. League average for position players is 100, and every point above 100 is a percentage point above league average. For example, a 125 wRC+ means a player is 25% better league average hitter. Similarly, every point below 100 is a percentage point below league average, so a 80 wRC+ means a player is 20% worse than the league average hitter. wRC+ also measures how a park effects a player's production for example, a player who plays in Colorado will have their wRC+ negatively impacted due to them playing in a hitter friendly park.

What is the difference between FWAR and BWAR?

The biggest difference between the two versions of WAR is how they calculate defensive value and pitching. For defense Fangraphs uses UZR(Ultimate Zone Rating) to calculate defense and Baseball Reference uses DRS(Defensive Runs Saved). Fangraphs values defense more than Baseball Reference. For pitchers Fangraphs calculates WAR by the pitchers FIP(Fielding Independent Pitching) and Baseball Reference uses RA9(Runs Allowed Per 9 Innings).
