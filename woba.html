wOBA

What is wOBA?

Weighted On-Base Average (wOBA) is a statistic used to to measure a hitter's overall offensive value, based on the relative values of each distinct offensive event. wOBA is based on a simple concept: Not all hits are created equal. Weighted On-Base Average combines all the different aspects of hitting into one metric, weighting each of them in proportion to their actual run value.

How is wOBA calculated?

To calculate wOBA, find the weights for the year you are interested in and multiply each weight by the player's corresponding statistics. For example, in 2013 Mike Trout had 100 walks, 9 HBP, 115 singles, 39 doubles, 9 triples, and 27 home runs. If you multiple each by it's corresponding weight and then divide that number by the sum of his at bats, walks (excluding Intentional Walks), hit by pitches, and sacrifice flies, you get .423, or his wOBA for the season.
